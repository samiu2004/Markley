About Makerly

Makerly is a Pakistan‑first freelance services marketplace prototype, created as a professional business project by Students of IBA.

Our mission

Enable Pakistani freelancers and small businesses to work together on a fair, transparent and modern platform with local payments, Urdu support and dual currency display.[web:120]

Makerly aims to reduce barriers like PayPal restrictions, high commissions and complex pricing structures common on global platforms.

Our positioning

Makerly is positioned as a focused alternative to Rozee.pk, Fiverr, Upwork and WorkChest, dedicated to services and projects rather than generic job listings.[web:87][web:295]

By concentrating on 10 core categories, the platform emphasizes quality over quantity and relevance for Pakistani clients.

Who built Makerly?

This prototype and business plan were designed and owned by Students of IBA as part of an academic project in entrepreneurship and digital business.

It demonstrates how a local marketplace could compete with international platforms through design, technology, and localized strategy.

Departments & responsibilities

The Makerly business plan organizes work into departments to run a full marketplace: Product & Technology, Marketing & Growth, and Operations / Finance / HR.

Product & Technology

Designs the platform, manages development, security, performance and future features like escrow, rating systems and advanced search.[web:134]

Ensures a mobile‑first, Urdu‑friendly and dual‑currency experience for users.

Marketing & Growth

Handles campaigns, university outreach, influencer marketing and social media content to bring freelancers and clients onto the platform.[web:123]

Tracks KPIs like signups, active users and cost per acquisition to optimize spend.

Operations, Finance & HR

Operations: manages verification, dispute handling and customer support workflows.[web:120]

Finance: monitors commission revenue and payouts. HR: recruits and supports the internal team growing Makerly.
