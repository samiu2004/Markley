How Makerly Works

Makerly connects Pakistani businesses and global clients with local freelancers through a simple, secure three-step process.[web:120]

1. Post or Find Work

Clients describe the project, budget and timeline, or search directly for freelancers in 10 focused categories such as web development, design, content and marketing.

Freelancers create profiles, list their skills, set rates in PKR, and optionally show approximate USD pricing for international clients.

2. Match, Chat & Agree

Clients review profiles, portfolios and ratings, then invite shortlisted freelancers to submit proposals. A built‑in messaging system (future feature) helps clarify scope and timelines.[web:134]

Once both sides agree on terms, the project is confirmed with a clear total price and Makerly’s 5% commission visible upfront.

3. Secure Work & Payment

Payment is held in escrow (planned feature) until the work is delivered. After completion, funds are released to the freelancer minus 5% commission.[web:120]

Local payout options like JazzCash, Easypaisa and bank transfer are prioritized to remove PayPal barriers for Pakistani freelancers.

Dual Currency & Urdu Support

Makerly is built for Pakistani users but stays friendly for international clients through dual currency and language options.

Dual currency view

  • Prices are set in PKR but can be previewed in USD for foreign clients.
  • Conversion uses a transparent reference rate (e.g. 1 USD ≈ 300 PKR) for display only.[web:240]
  • Clients and freelancers avoid confusion when agreeing on budgets.

This approach helps Pakistani freelancers communicate value in both local and international terms without changing how they actually get paid.

Urdu interface elements

Key labels and flows can be switched to Urdu so non‑English speakers can still use the marketplace comfortably.

  • Buttons and headings (e.g. “Hire Talent”, “Post Project”).
  • Navigation labels and helper text.
  • Onboarding tips and verification steps.

This prototype includes a basic EN / اردو toggle for key headings, showing how a bilingual interface would work.

Behind the scenes: Departments

Makerly’s business plan is structured around focused departments to run a professional marketplace: Product/Tech, Marketing, Operations, Finance and HR.

Product & Technology

Builds and maintains the platform, including matching, search, escrow logic, analytics and security controls.

Responsible for mobile‑first design and continuous improvements based on user feedback.

Marketing & Growth

Runs campaigns, influencer partnerships, social media and university outreach to bring in both freelancers and clients.

Owns Makerly’s brand as a Pakistan‑first alternative to global marketplaces.

Operations, Finance & HR

Operations: Manages verification, dispute resolution and customer support workflows.

Finance: Tracks commission revenue, payouts and budgets. HR: Hires and supports the internal Makerly team.
